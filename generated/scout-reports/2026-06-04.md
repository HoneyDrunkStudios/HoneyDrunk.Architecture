## Ranked Opportunities

### 1. Agent Governance / Audit Evidence Wrapper

- **Thesis:** For regulated .NET shops running MCP/tool-using AI agents, provide an attributable, queryable audit record of model calls, tool calls, approvals, costs, and outcomes.
- **Buyer:** Engineering lead or platform owner at a 20-to-80-person regulated .NET SaaS company experimenting with Claude Code, Copilot agents, OpenAI Agents SDK, or MCP servers in production workflows.
- **Smallest viable wedge:** A hosted audit-ingest endpoint plus .NET/MCP adapter that records tool calls into the Audit substrate and renders a "who touched what with which tool and cost" report.
- **Build cost:** ~8+ solo-dev weeks after Audit Phase 1 and AI cost-ledger work are actually available; substantially more if Audit remains Seed.
- **Opportunity cost:** Pulls time away from Notify Cloud, HoneyHub dispatch architecture, and Container Apps deployment. It would also split the commercial narrative before the first commercial trial has signal.
- **Kill criteria:** Do not promote before Notify Cloud produces transferable .NET-shop signal, Audit Phase 1 reaches a stable release, and a Critic pass confirms buyers will pay for audit retention rather than generic observability.
- **Why HoneyDrunk:** The Grid's Audit invariant separates durable record-of-truth from telemetry, which is a cleaner substrate than retrofitting audit onto observability.
- **Disposition:** Park. Already captured by PDR-0010; no packet.

### 2. Notify Cloud Deliverability-Safe Dev Wedge

- **Thesis:** For indie .NET developers and small SaaS shops, a .NET-native notification API with email/SMS routing and decision logs remains the best near-term external product trial.
- **Buyer:** Indie .NET SaaS developer or 2-to-5-person .NET team that currently glues transactional email, SMS, retries, rate limits, and notification preferences by hand.
- **Smallest viable wedge:** Complete Notify/Pulse dev deployment, accept Notify Cloud standup, then ship the kickoff slice around tenant isolation, API-key validation, and SDK shape.
- **Build cost:** Already in the active queue; incremental Scout-created work would not reduce scope.
- **Opportunity cost:** Any additional opportunistic packet here competes with the already-ranked deployment and kickoff work rather than clarifying it.
- **Kill criteria:** If the kickoff slice cannot preserve the internal Notify path, or the soft-launch waitlist cannot show credible .NET developer interest after the deployment blocker clears, drop Notify Cloud to maintenance/dogfood rather than expanding scope.
- **Why HoneyDrunk:** Notify, Communications, Vault, Web.Rest, Auth, Pulse, and Container Apps deployment work all get production pressure from one product.
- **Disposition:** Stay the current course. Existing PDR-0002 / ADR-0027 work covers it; no packet.

### 3. Curiosities Launch-District Content Spike

- **Thesis:** For curious city walkers, a curated one-district discovery loop can test whether "walk to question marks, uncover your city" is more compelling than generic map search.
- **Buyer:** Local explorers and travelers who like curated walks, public art, history, Atlas Obscura-style places, and artifact-like trip memories.
- **Smallest viable wedge:** Pick one launch district and produce ~25 reviewed POIs from open data + AI-assisted enrichment, then test real walks before mobile investment.
- **Build cost:** ~2 solo-dev weeks for a strict content spike, but follow-on mobile/prototype work is much larger.
- **Opportunity cost:** Pulls attention from Q2 infrastructure and product-thread commitments. The current roadmap already places Curiosities Phase 0 in Q2/Q3 without displacing Notify Cloud.
- **Kill criteria:** If 25 reviewed POIs cannot be produced at acceptable quality and safety within the spike window, or if testers browse but do not physically walk to unlocks, do not scale to app work.
- **Why HoneyDrunk:** Lore/Knowledge patterns and the studio's aesthetic can make curated discovery feel authored rather than directory-like.
- **Disposition:** Park until the existing roadmap slot. PDR-0008 already names the packet; no duplicate.
