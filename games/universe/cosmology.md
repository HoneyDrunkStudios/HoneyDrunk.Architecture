# Universe — Cosmology (What Is Scarce)

**Status:** Seed (living document; canon accretes — keep loose)
**Last updated:** 2026-06-20

What everyone in the world is competing *for*, and why it's scarce. This is the keystone that explains the contest-for-power premise (`overview.md`). Per the 2026-06-20 design conversation.

## The model: essence

There is a single underlying substance — call it **essence** (name TBD) — that is the world's universal currency:

- It **powers abilities** (the basis of the power system).
- It **sustains life**.
- It **feeds the higher beings** (entities/deities draw on it, e.g. through worship).

This unifies the world's ecology, religion, economy, and power system into one scarce thing.

### Essence is plural, not monolithic

There are **types** of essence (specific types TBD — do not lock yet). Crucial properties:

- Some beings are **born with** certain types; others **lack** types they need or want.
- This makes **acquisition the engine of the world** — every being chases the types it lacks. Trade, theft, conquest, pacts, and monster-predation are all just *different methods of acquiring essence*. One idea, many expressions.
- Typed essence is the structural backbone of the power system (affinities, domains, rock-paper-scissors-style interactions in the HxH/JJK/Naruto tradition). See `power-system.md`.

### Acquisition paths (keep the climb primary)

Essence (and the power it grants) can be obtained by:

1. **Earning it** — understanding the rules, training: *the climb*. **← primary path; must always be viable on its own.**
2. **Taking it** — combat, hunting monsters.
3. **Being granted it** — by deities/entities via pacts/favor. **← optional layer, never the only way.**

> Guardrail (PDR-0012 §C / pact mechanic): borrowed/granted power must **not** be the core. A player should always be able to become powerful *without* pacts, so pacts stay flavor, not a crutch.

## How the menu options folded together

The cosmology synthesizes four candidate "scarce things" rather than picking one:

- **(A) Essence as universal currency** — the base model above.
- **(C) Favor of higher beings** — folds in: deities are *sources* of essence, so favor becomes a *route to acquisition*, not a separate economy.
- **(D) Knowledge of the rules** — folds in: understanding essence is the *method* of earning/wielding it (the climb).
- **(B) Territory & safety** — kept as a **second axis**: the ordinary majority don't compete for essence at all; they compete for *safe, livable, controlled ground* (`overview.md`, two-tier society). Geopolitics of space sits alongside the geopolitics of essence.

## Monsters fit cleanly

Man-eating monsters eat people **for essence** — not malice, metabolism. They may **embody** specific essence types and compete for **territory** too. Fully consistent with grey morality (`bestiary.md`).

## Open questions (resolve as games force them)

- The name of essence; whether it has a public/common name vs. an esoteric true name.
- The number and identity of essence *types*.
- Whether essence is finite world-wide, regenerating, or extractable from specific sources (gates? deities? the dead?).
- How deity "feeding" on essence actually works and what it costs mortals.
