# Universe — Overview

**Status:** Seed (living document; canon accretes — keep loose)
**Last updated:** 2026-06-20

The shared fictional universe behind HoneyDrunk's in-universe games. This is the *constant* across an anthology of games with different protagonists, eras, and genres. Per PDR-0012.

> Naming: the universe itself is unnamed (TBD). Everything below is a load-bearing *principle*, not locked detail.

## The premise (the engine of the world)

The world runs on a single, endless **contest for power** — every actor (individuals, factions, monsters, entities, deities) is trying to rise in power, influence, wealth, and resources. This contest, not a "good vs. evil" axis, is the engine that generates conflict everywhere for free.

"Good vs. evil" exists only as a **lens characters apply** to the contest — never as the world's structure. Morality is grey and **perspective-dependent**:

> A man-eating monster isn't evil; it's a species eating to live, raise young, and hold territory. To humans it's a predator; to the monster, humans are the invasive apex predator. **Every faction — including monsters — has a coherent worldview in which they are the reasonable ones.** Hold this line consistently and the grey morality writes itself.

## The two-tier society (why the world feels real)

Not everyone competes. This is the key to making the urban-fantasy grounding *work*:

- **The ordinary majority** don't chase power — they want *safety and stability*: a normal life, a normal job, a safe city. (Their scarce resource is **safety/territory** — see `cosmology.md` option B.)
- **The climbers** — a dangerous minority — compete for power, and their contest *endangers everyone else*.

This gives every in-universe game two registers (grounded normal world ↔ hidden/dangerous power-world) and gives a "starts extremely weak" protagonist a real arc: they begin as one of the ordinary, and *crossing over* into the contest is the inciting moment.

## Setting shape

- A grounded **modern-city** baseline, with passage into other **realms/dimensions** (loose Solo-Leveling "gates" vibe — *not* a literal mechanic).
- Monsters, entities, deities, and dimensions are real and woven into how power works.
- The world is meant to feel **vast and fully traversable** — mountain peak to seafloor, every terrain (a dream-game-scale ambition; smaller games show slices).

## Anthology continuity

Because protagonists change between games, continuity is carried by:
- The **world, its history, geography, and factions**
- The **power-system rules**
- The **entities & deities** — immortal, so they persist across eras and become the recurring "cast." A being met as a terrifying boss in one game can reappear, centuries later, in another. (See `pantheon.md`.)
