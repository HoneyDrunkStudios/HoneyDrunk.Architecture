# Universe — Entities & Deities (The Recurring Cast)

**Status:** Seed (living document; canon accretes — keep loose)
**Last updated:** 2026-06-20

The higher beings of the universe. Because the anthology uses a **new protagonist per game** (PDR-0012 §C), the entities and deities are the **continuity device** — they're immortal, so they persist across eras and become the recurring "cast" that threads otherwise-separate games together.

## Role in the world

- **Sources of essence.** Deities/entities can grant essence (or specific types), making their **favor a route to acquisition** (`cosmology.md`). They feed on essence in turn (e.g. through worship).
- **Active players in the contest.** They're not set dressing — they compete for power, influence, and followers like everyone else (`overview.md` premise).
- **Pact partners.** Bonds with entities grant *borrowed power* — an optional layer, never the core (guardrail in `cosmology.md`).
- **Domains.** Each may govern certain essence types / spheres (to be designed).

## Continuity device (the payoff of anthology)

A being encountered as a terrifying **boss** in game 2 can reappear — worshipped, feared, or scheming — **centuries later** in the dream game. Players who met it once carry that recognition across titles. This only works *because* protagonists change. Lean into it.

## Grey morality applies here too

Deities are not "good" or "evil" — they pursue their own survival, influence, and ends. A deity beloved by one faction is a tyrant to another (`factions.md`). Worship is partly *transactional* (essence flows both ways).

## Open questions (resolve as games force them)

- How many tiers of being (lesser entities → greater entities → deities)? Where's the line?
- Origin: were they always immortal? born of essence? something else?
- Can mortals ascend? (Ties directly to the "climb" fantasy and the dream game's endgame.)
- Naming, domains, and the first 1–2 concrete entities to introduce in game 2.
